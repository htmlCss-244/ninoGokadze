Italian Wine

Italy has produced wine for thousands of years, making it one of the world's oldest wine-producing countries. From alpine valleys to sun-drenched islands, Italy offers one of the most varied wine landscapes on the planet. With centuries of tradition and hundreds of native grape varieties, Italian wine culture is a tapestry of innovation, heritage, and regional pride.

The Three Most Popular Wine Regions

Tuscany

The Tuscany wine region is one of the most iconic wine destinations in Italy

Piedmont

In Italy’s northwestern corner, Piedmont stands among the country’s most prestigious wine regions, and it’s one of the standout areas of Northern Italy for world-class reds.

Veneto

The Veneto wine region is one of Italy’s largest and most versatile regions, offering something for every palate.

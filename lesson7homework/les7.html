The Highest Mountains

The highest mountains in the world are led by Mount Everest, located in the Himalayas on the border between Nepal and China. Its height is 8,849 meters, making it the tallest peak in the world

The second and third highest are Kangchenjunga and Lhotse, also in the Himalayas. Kangchenjunga is 8,586 meters tall, while Lhotse reaches 8,516 meters. All these peaks are notable not only for their height but also for extreme conditions and challenges for climbers.


Mountain Name Height (m) Location
Mount Everest 8,849 Himalayas, Nepal/China
Kangchenjunga 8,586 Himalayas, Nepal/India
Lhotse 8,516 Himalayas, Nepal/China
